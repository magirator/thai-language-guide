The Essentials
5 Non-Negotiable
Rules of Thai Grammar

Thai grammar is remarkably logical. These 5 rules cover the foundation of everything you'll say and hear.

Rule 01
No verb conjugation
Thai verbs never change. "I eat / he eats / they ate / we will eat" = กิน (gin) every single time. Time is shown by adding time words, not changing the verb.
ผมกิน · คุณกิน · เขากิน — all the same verb
Rule 02
Subject + Verb + Object
Sentence order is the same as English. ผม + กิน + ข้าว = I eat rice. Keep this order and you will be understood.
ฉัน + ชอบ + อาหารไทย = I like Thai food
Rule 03
Polite particles end sentences
Men add ครับ (khrap). Women add ค่ะ / คะ (kha). These make you sound respectful and natural instantly. Use them constantly.
ขอบคุณครับ · สวัสดีค่ะ
Rule 04
Negation = ไม่ before the verb
Place ไม่ (mai) directly before any verb or adjective to negate it. That's the only rule — no tense changes needed.
ผมกิน → ผมไม่กิน (I eat → I don't eat)
Rule 05
Questions use particles
Add ไหม (mai) at the end for yes/no questions. Use question words (อะไร, ที่ไหน, ใคร…) at the end of the sentence for open questions.
คุณชอบไหม? / คุณชอบอะไร?
Master Patterns
6 Sentence Structures
That Cover Everything

Learn these 6 patterns and you can build thousands of sentences. Every structure includes a formula and ready-to-use examples.

👤

Structure 1 — "I am / I am not"

Introduce yourself, describe identity, profession, nationality

✦ Subject + เป็น (pen) + noun → "is / am / are"
✦ Subject + ไม่ใช่ (mai chai) + noun → "is not / am not"
ThaiPhoneticEnglish
ผมเป็นคนอเมริกันPhom pen khon AmerikaI am American
ฉันเป็นครูChan pen khruuI am a teacher
ผมไม่ใช่คนไทยPhom mai chai khon ThaiI am not Thai
คุณเป็นใคร?Khun pen khai?Who are you?
🙋

Structure 2 — "I want / I need"

Request things, express desires, order food, ask for help

✦ Subject + ต้องการ (tong gan) + noun → formal want/need
✦ Subject + อยาก (yak) + verb → casual "want to do"
ThaiPhoneticEnglish
ผมต้องการน้ำPhom tong gan namI want / need water
ฉันอยากกินข้าวChan yak gin khaoI want to eat rice
ผมไม่อยาก...Phom mai yak...I don't want to...
คุณอยากได้อะไร?Khun yak dai arai?What do you want?
❤️

Structure 3 — "I like / I don't like"

Express preferences, opinions, taste

✦ Subject + ชอบ (chop) + noun/verb → "like"
✦ Subject + ไม่ชอบ (mai chop) + noun/verb → "don't like"
ThaiPhoneticEnglish
ผมชอบอาหารไทยPhom chop ahan ThaiI like Thai food
ฉันไม่ชอบเผ็ดChan mai chop phetI don't like spicy
คุณชอบอะไร?Khun chop arai?What do you like?
ชอบมากเลยChop mak loeiI really like it
✅

Structure 4 — "Can / Can't"

Express ability, ask for permission, offer help

✦ Subject + verb + ได้ (dai) → can do
✦ Subject + verb + ไม่ได้ (mai dai) → can't do
ThaiPhoneticEnglish
ผมพูดภาษาไทยได้นิดหน่อยPhom phut phasa Thai dai nit noiI can speak a little Thai
ผมพูดภาษาไทยไม่ได้Phom phut phasa Thai mai daiI can't speak Thai
คุณพูดภาษาอังกฤษได้ไหม?Khun phut phasa Angkrit dai mai?Can you speak English?
ช่วยผมได้ไหม?Chuay phom dai mai?Can you help me?
❓

These go at the END of the sentence in Thai (unlike English)

Structure 6 — Past, Present, Future

No verb changes. Just add a time marker before or after the verb.

TimeThaiPhoneticExample
Already (past)แล้วlaeoกินแล้ว = already ate
Just nowเพิ่งphoengเพิ่งมา = just arrived
Right nowกำลังgamlangกำลังกิน = eating now
Will (future)จะjaจะไป = will go
Yesterdayเมื่อวานmuea wanเมื่อวานไป = went yesterday
Tomorrowพรุ่งนี้phrung niพรุ่งนี้จะไป = will go tomorrow
