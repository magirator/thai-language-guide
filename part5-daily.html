Express Routine

For extremely busy days. Keeps momentum alive — never miss two days in a row.

0–5 min
Flashcard Review
Open Anki. Review all due cards only. Say every answer out loud — never just read it in your head.
5–10 min
Listening Input
Play a Thai podcast or YouTube video at your level. Don't stress about understanding everything — just absorb the sounds and rhythm.
10–15 min
Speaking Output
Say 10 sentences out loud using today's topic. No notes. Self-correct immediately. Record yourself if you can.
15–20 min
5 New Words Only
Add exactly 5 new words to Anki from the 1000-word list. No more on busy days — consistency over quantity.
30
min

Standard Routine — Recommended

The daily baseline. Do this every day and you'll be conversational in 30 weeks.

0–7 min
Flashcard Review
Anki — all due cards. Speak every answer out loud. If you hesitate even a little, mark it "again." No cheating yourself.
7–12 min
Listening
ThaiPod101 dialogue or Thai YouTube (food, travel, lifestyle content works great). Listen twice: once for gist, once for specific words.
12–20 min
Speaking Simulation
Pick one script from Part 3. Play both roles out loud. Then improvise: change one detail (different food order, different destination). Record yourself and listen back — note one mistake to fix tomorrow.
20–27 min
New Vocabulary (10 words)
Learn 10 new words from the 1000-word list. Write them phonetically. Use each one in a sentence out loud. Add to Anki.
27–30 min
Revision Sprint
Review yesterday's 10 words and last week's grammar structure. One sentence each. Quick and done.
45
min

Deep Dive

For motivated days and weekends. This is where big leaps happen.

0–5 min
Thai Warm-Up
Greet yourself in Thai. Describe what you can see around you in Thai. Force output from the very first minute of your day.
5–12 min
Flashcard Review
All Anki due cards — no skipping. Speak every answer. If a card has been wrong 3 times, write the word out by hand.
12–20 min
Listening + Transcript
Watch Thai YouTube WITH Thai subtitles. Pause on unknown words. Write them down. Do not use English subtitles — that defeats the purpose.
20–30 min
Full Conversation Simulation
Do a complete script from Part 3. Then fully improvise a version — change the location, the food, the price. Record yourself. Notice the hesitations.
30–38 min
New Grammar Structure
Study one new structure from Part 2. Write 5 original sentences using it. They should be about your real life, not made-up scenarios.
38–43 min
New Vocabulary (15 words)
Add 15 words to Anki. Chunk by topic — don't mix random categories. Topic-chunked words stick together in your memory.
43–45 min
Thai Reflection
In Thai, answer: "What did I learn today? What was hard?" Even 2 sentences counts. This closes the loop on the session.

From the Tutor
7 Things I Tell
Every Student

After 10+ years of teaching, these are the insights that separate students who become fluent from those who plateau.

Tip 01
Speak on Day 1
Your accent will not be perfect. That's fine. Thais love that you try and will help you. Waiting until you "know enough" is the #1 mistake — there is no enough, only practice.
Tip 02
Don't skip the tones
A wrong tone changes the word entirely. Spend 5 minutes on tones every morning in the first month. Say the 5 tones with "ma" daily. This pays off for years.
Tip 03
Use ไม่เป็นไร constantly
"Mai pen rai" means it's fine / no problem / never mind. It's the most culturally Thai phrase that exists. Use it and watch Thais light up around you.
Tip 04
ครับ / ค่ะ more than you think
End every sentence with these polite particles. Thais notice immediately when learners use them correctly — it signals respect and makes every interaction smoother.
Tip 05
Mass input after 300 words
Once you have 300+ words, start consuming Thai content daily. Even understanding 20% is training your ear. Thai Netflix, music, YouTube — 20 minutes a day accelerates everything.
Tip 06
Speaking > Reading
10 minutes of speaking beats 1 hour of reading flashcards. Thai is a spoken language first. Prioritize your mouth and ears over your eyes, especially in the first 3 months.
Tip 07
Get a Thai friend
Nothing replaces a real conversation partner. italki tutors, Tandem app partners, or simply talking to Thai people around you — this single step is your fastest path to fluency.
