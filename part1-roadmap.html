The Journey
Four Phases to
Conversational Thai

Thai grammar is simpler than English — no verb conjugation, no plurals, no gender. The main challenges are tones and script. This roadmap skips theory and gets you speaking fast.

1

Survival Thai

Weeks 1–4 · Say who you are, ask questions, survive daily situations

Week Focus What You Can Do
Week 1 Tones + phonetics, greetings, numbers 1–100 Greet anyone, count, say your name
Week 2 Yes/no questions, "I want / I like / I don't want" Order food, buy things
Week 3 Location words, asking where things are Navigate, ask for directions
Week 4 Time expressions, daily routine phrases Describe your day
Have a 2-minute introduction conversation entirely in Thai — without notes.
2

Core Communication

Weeks 5–10 · Handle most everyday situations

Week Focus What You Can Do
Week 5 Past / present / future time markers Talk about what happened, what you'll do
Week 6 Feelings, opinions, adjectives Express emotions and preferences
Week 7 Shopping and negotiating Buy anything at a market
Week 8 Food and restaurant conversations Order complex meals, ask about ingredients
Week 9 Transport — taxi, bus, BTS, songthaew Get anywhere in Thailand
Week 10 Health and emergencies Handle basic health situations
Spend 30 minutes in a Thai market using only Thai.
3

Conversational Fluency

Weeks 11–20 · Hold real conversations, understand replies

Weeks Focus What You Can Do
11–12 Fillers, softeners, particles (นะ, ครับ, เลย) Sound natural, not robotic
13–14 Thai culture — polite speech, social topics Navigate social situations gracefully
15–16 Storytelling — connecting sentences, past events Tell stories, explain things
17–18 Work and daily life vocabulary Discuss jobs, routines, lifestyle
19–20 Thai script — basic reading (menus, signs) Read real-world Thai
Have a 15-minute unscripted conversation with a native speaker.
4

Expansion

Weeks 21–30 · Handle unexpected topics, understand fast speech

Weeks Focus What You Can Do
21–23 Slang, informal speech, social media Thai Talk like a real person, not a textbook
24–26 Thai TV, music, YouTube without subtitles Comprehend native speed
27–30 Thai script reading and basic writing Full literacy foundation
Watch a Thai TV show and understand 70%+ without subtitles.
